**Session – B2C Sales / Lead pipeline reporting**

Goal (rough): clear view of performance across pipeline

- lead volume over time
- conversion through funnel
- lead source / campaign performance
- speed (time to contact / time to sale)
- revenue / outcomes

Attendees: Raj (Sales), Sophie (Marketing), James (Data), Lisa (CRM), + me taking notes

**Lead definition**

Sophie: lead = anyone showing intent (form fills, campaign responses etc)

“we need ALL leads for marketing reporting”

Raj pushed back hard on that
said that’s not a lead, that’s just interest

“lead = ready to be called”

James mentioned warehouse has prospect / lead / opportunity but… not consistent
some systems just call everything “contact”

note to self: no shared definition here at all
this is going to break pretty much everything downstream (volume, conversion etc)

**Lead volume (what are we even counting?)**

Asked: what does “lead volume over time” actually mean?

Sophie - all inbound leads (otherwise marketing looks bad / invisible)

Raj - only dialler-ready leads
otherwise “numbers meaningless”

same metric, completely different meaning depending on who you ask

**Funnel / conversion (two completely different worlds)**

Marketing funnel (Sophie talking through this quickly):
impression -> click -> form fill -> lead -> qualified -> sale

Sales funnel (Raj):
lead -> attempted -> contacted -> quoted -> won/lost

Lisa: but the CRM doesn’t even track impressions/clicks

attempted calls not always logged

James: linking marketing clicks -> actual sales = unreliable (why?)

Raj wants: lead -> sale conversion by source
Sophie: says that ignores campaign performance entirely

**Lead source / campaign stuff**

Sophie: need reporting by campaign + channel

Raj: lots of leads just say “web” - useless

James: ~14 different “source” fields across systems (??)

Lisa: some get overwritten during processing

(note: this feels like a complete mess structurally)

**Speed / responsiveness**

Raj: “speed to call is critical” (within 5 mins)

I asked: can we do lead creation -> first contact time?

James: not reliably (timestamps missing / bad)

Lisa: dialler has call times but CRM doesn’t always capture first attempt

so metric exists conceptually but data isn’t there cleanly

**Ownership**

Raj: once it’s a lead -> sales owns it

Sophie: marketing still nurtures leads after creation

Lisa: ownership field changes depending on stage

not consistent at all

**Data quality (bit of frustration here)**

Raj: duplicates everywhere, same customer called multiple times

Sophie: marketing platform does dedupe

James: rules differ between systems

(so… not really deduped in reality)

**random comment (can't remember who said this exactly but important!)**

“sometimes we reclassify leads as opportunities if they look good”

sounds like manual overrides happening
not part of any defined process

**end-of-session thoughts (scribbled after)**
no shared funnel definition
no agreed “lead” definition
data model doesn’t match how people talk about the business
key KPIs (conversion, speed) not actually measurable with current data
multiple systems overlapping / conflicting
